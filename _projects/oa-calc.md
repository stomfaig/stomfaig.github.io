It is easy enough to consider the sine, cosine or exponential of a real number. But can we do the same to a matrix? When does is make sense? These articles are intended to give some sort of an answer to such questions.  
In this series we will work in Banach and C\*-algebras, and investigate when can we define what it means to compute certain functions of an element. For polynomials we have an easy enough job: if $A$ is a Banach algebra, and $a \in A$, it makes perfect sense to apply the function $f(x) = x^2$ to $a$:
<center>$$f(a) = a \cdot a$$</center>
But how far can we take this? Can we also take the square root of $a$? These articles will try to give an explanation, and exhibit the limitations one might face, if the algebra $A$ is not sufficiently well-behaved.
